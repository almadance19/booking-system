üèÜ Core Philosophy

"Building confidence and community through exceptional technical training."

üéØ Audience Strategy Framework

üî• Warm Private Audience

Who: Current students, email list, referrals

  • Highest conversion rate (15-25%)
  • Lowest acquisition cost
  • Highest lifetime value potential

üî• Warm Public Audience

Who: Social media followers, website visitors, past students

  • Medium conversion rate (5-15%)
  • Know your brand but haven't committed
  • Engaged but not activated

‚ùÑÔ∏è Cold Private Audience

Who: Referral leads, community contacts, partnerships

  • Medium-high conversion rate (8-18%)
  • Social proof lowers resistance
  • Trust established through connection

‚ùÑÔ∏è Cold Public Audience

Who: Paid ad targets, local search, walk-ins

  • Lowest conversion rate (1-5%)
  • Highest acquisition cost
  • No prior relationship exists

üìà The Core Four Marketing Framework

1. ACQUISITION: Getting Strangers to Know You

Goal: Generate leads at the lowest possible cost

üî• Warm Private Audience

  • Referral Program: "Refer a Friend: You both get one month free"
  • Email Sequences: Win-back campaigns for inactive students
  • Internal Upsell: "Sibling Enrollment Special"

üî• Warm Public Audience

  • Social Media Retargeting: Show ads to engaged followers
  • Lead Magnet: "5 Common Dance Mistakes" free video series
  • Webinar/Workshop: Live Q&A with head instructor

‚ùÑÔ∏è Cold Private Audience

  • Partnership Referrals: Local schools, pediatricians, sports clubs
  • Community Events: Library workshops, school demos
  • Networking: Parent groups, local business associations

‚ùÑÔ∏è Cold Public Audience

  • Hyper-Local Facebook/Instagram Ads: Target parents within 5-mile radius
  • Google Search Ads: "dance classes near me" keywords
  • Local SEO: Optimize for "best dance school in [city]"

üéÅ The "Hero" Free Offer (For All Audiences)

"Confidence on the Floor" Starter Workshop: A free 45-minute workshop for absolute beginners with a promised "first win" - one simple combination they can show off immediately.

2. ACTIVATION: Turning Prospects into First-Time Paid Students

Goal: Get leads to experience their "First Win" and pay for first core offering

üî• Warm Private Audience

  • Personal Invitation: "As a valued community member, we're reserving a spot for you"
  • Early Access: 48-hour priority registration before public
  • Testimonial Integration: Show similar students' success stories

üî• Warm Public Audience

  • Limited-Time Bonus: "Enroll this week and get free dance shoes rental"
  • Social Proof: Share transformation stories from similar beginners
  • Webinar to Course Funnel: Special offer at end of free workshop

‚ùÑÔ∏è Cold Private Audience

  • Partner Discount: "Mention [Partner Name] for 15% off first course"
  • Success Session: One-on-one goal setting call (not sales call)
  • Risk Reversal: "Money-back guarantee if no confidence after 4 weeks"

‚ùÑÔ∏è Cold Public Audience

  • Clear Next Step: "First Steps" 8-Week Fundamentals Course ($199)
  • Simplified Choice: Only one beginner option presented initially
  • Immediate Onboarding: Book first class within 24 hours of sign-up

üéØ The Irresistible Core Offer

"First Steps" 8-Week Fundamentals Course: Fixed-length, progressive curriculum with tangible outcome: "Dance in our student showcase" or "Master our 8-week curriculum."

3. MONETIZATION: Making More Money From Existing Students

Goal: Increase Lifetime Value (LTV) before they think of leaving

üî• Warm Private Audience

  • Unlimited Membership: Primary offer after 8-week course ($129/month)
  • Elite Programs: Invitation-only competition teams
  • Family Packages: Discount for multiple family members

üî• Warm Public Audience

  • Masterclass Series: Quarterly workshops with guest instructors ($99/event)
  • Specialty Workshops: "Parent-Child Dance Weekends"
  • Digital Products: "Practice at Home" video library subscription

‚ùÑÔ∏è Cold Private Audience

  • Semi-Private Coaching: Small-group "Spotlight Sessions" ($50/session)
  • Event Packages: Wedding dance choreography packages
  • Corporate Programs: Team-building dance workshops

‚ùÑÔ∏è Cold Public Audience

  • Performance Packages: Recital costumes + video ($199-399)
  • Merchandise: Branded apparel fostering community pride
  • Downsell Option: 4-Class Punch Card for limited commitment

4. RETENTION: Keeping Students For Years

Goal: Eliminate churn by creating unmatched value and community

üî• Warm Private Audience

  • Loyalty Program: "Dance Journey Rewards" - milestones earn perks
  • Student Leadership Path: Assistant ‚Üí Junior Teacher roles
  • Alumni Rate: Special pricing for returning graduates

üî• Warm Public Audience

  • Private Community: Facebook group with exclusive content
  • Progress Tracking: Digital skill badges and certificates
  • Monthly Highlights: "Dancer of the Month" social features

‚ùÑÔ∏è Cold Private Audience

  • Personal Check-ins: Quarterly "progress review" calls
  • Family Involvement: Parent observation weeks with feedback sessions
  • Milestone Celebrations: Physical cards for dance anniversaries

‚ùÑÔ∏è Cold Public Audience

  • Email Nurture: Weekly "win" updates and educational content
  • Social Proof: Regular student transformation stories
  • Event Calendar: Clear yearly schedule of performances and socials

ü§ù Retention Superpowers

  • Community Events: Student socials, parent mixers, watch parties
  • Progress Recognition: Annual progress videos sent to parents/students
  • Alumni Network: Connect current students with graduates in college dance programs

üìä Implementation Timeline & Metrics

Quarter 1: Foundation & Retention Focus

  • Build free workshop and 8-week course curriculum
  • Implement referral program and one community event
  • Key Metric: Student Retention Rate ‚Üë 10%

Quarter 2: Acquisition & Activation Launch

  • Run first free workshop cycle
  • Launch hyper-local ads to cold audiences
  • Formalize 2 school partnerships
  • Key Metrics: Cost Per Lead & Activation Rate

Quarter 3: Monetization Expansion

  • Launch premium masterclass weekends
  • Introduce performance packages for holiday recital
  • Test merchandise line
  • Key Metric: Average Revenue Per Student

Quarter 4: Systemization & Planning

  • Systemize successful processes into checklists
  • Plan next annual calendar around this cycle
  • Analyze full year data
  • Key Metric: Lifetime Value

üí° Final Hormozi Principle

"Double down on what works and cut what doesn't."

Track where your best students come from (which referral source? which partnership?) and pour your energy and resources there. Your dance school isn't just a service; it's a community and a transformation. Market the transformation, and the students will fill your studio.

üìù Implementation Notes

  1. Start with the audience that's easiest to convert (Warm Private)
  2. Build systems for each audience type before scaling
  3. Measure everything - what gets measured gets improved
  4. Focus on customer lifetime value, not just initial sign-up
  5. Community building is your #1 retention tool
